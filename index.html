What is Sunderfolk

Rediscover Game Night with the magic of Sunderfolk, a shared turn-based tactical RPG adventure where your mobile device becomes the controller. Built with a couch co-op experience in mind, up to four players adventure through the magical and dangerous Sunderlands.

Sunderfolk + DLC (2023 - 2025)

Summarized Responsibilities:

  • Owned end-to-end design of 10 characters (1 playable hero, 4 bosses, 5 enemies), translating each one's fantasy and role into distinct ability kit, passive, and AI behavior, from concept through ship on PC, Xbox, PS5, and Switch.
  • Designed and shipped 10+ levels from paper design and blockout through final polish, anchoring each on a distinct mechanic (puzzle, combat, exploration) that promote players toward cooperative strategy.
  • Wrote and maintained design documentation for every shipped character and level (intended player experience, encounter flow, anchoring mechanic) keeping Engineering, Art, and QA aligned through iteration.
  • Collaborated with the Animation Lead to drive narrative and visual direction for 30+ cinematic cutscenes; translated gameplay moments into story beats, storyboards, and art briefs that delivered cohesive storytelling on tight release schedules.
  • Led 3+ internal playtests weekly, turning qualitative feedback and edge-case findings into concrete design changes.

COMBAT DESIGN

Hero
VANGUARD

Born for battle, the Vanguard pushes deep into enemy lines where their abilities burn brightest. Small yet mighty, they stand as a fierce contender to the Berserker.

For each Grit, the Vanguard takes 1 less damage when attacked and deals 2 damage to the attacker, reinforcing their role as both a frontliner and a DPS.

Into the Fray

As the first spell available to the player, Into the Fray teaches the Vanguard's unique keyword and the Grit mechanic.

Bladefall

One of the 3 early spells, Bladefall reinforces the Vanguard's role as frontliner by granting extra damage for each adjacent enemy.

Boss Monster
CHAMPION

This charismatic muscle-bound Champion has big plans for the Hero. Trained in the art of the Muscle, they present a unique challenge that forces players to approach the fight differently from their usual high-damage strategies.

With damage capped at 1, raw output won't win this fight — players must turn the Champion's own abilities and the environment against them.

Bombs Away

A ranged AoE whose damage scales with each minion, keeping crowd control crucial.

Spread out to minimize damage. The blast also leaves objects players can use against the Champion.

Galvanize

Past a certain threshold, the Champion summons additional minions. The result is a fun interplay between the boss's abilities, the crowd, and the narrative.

Monster
WURM

A subterranean ambusher, the Tunnel Wurm strikes from beneath the hex grid with a crushing double-bite. Reposition or pay the price.

Swallow the World

Unique to the Wurm, this ability forcefully repositions every character in range, dragging them through hazardous terrain and objects such as pits and boomshrooms before unleashing a powerful attack.

Devour

Powerful and dangerous, this spell makes the Wurm a significant threat while also serving as a useful tool against other monsters.

ENCOUNTER & QUEST DESIGN

Tavern Trouble

Type: Tutorial

This tutorial teaches the game's basic controls, how your phone becomes your controller, and your role in the Sunderlands.

Sacred Throne

Type: Boss Battle

Help the Sacred Bug reclaim their throne from the game's first multi-hex Boss!

Rubble Maze

Type: Puzzle and Battle

Face the Wurm, Sunderfolk's toughest enemy, in a maze where falling rocks reshape the board — forcing new tactics at every turn.

Brightstone Cleanup

Type: Exploration

Take down the Brightstone lamp in the game's most dangerous encounter, facing some of its strongest enemies.

DESIGN PROCESS

Ideation

Design goals:

  • Set up the narrative premise for the DLC campaign.
  • Introduce the new Ally and Monster.
  • Offer a fresh experience or strategy uncommon to the main game.

From there, define the encounter's map shape, quest, and objectives; build the enemy lineup and their starting positions; and craft a narrative that supports and enriches it.

Paper Prototype and Implementation

Run paper playtests virtually in Miro, then gather feedback to refine every element — from the Ally and Monster to objectives and starting positions.

Build the first in-engine version and produce a playable prototype for full-team playtests.

Iteration and Polish

Debug code, wire up animation and VFX hookups, and write the encounter's narrative and tutorials. Then keep gathering feedback and iterating as more team-wide playtests are run.
